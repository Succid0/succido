The Sun

The Sun is the star at the center of our solar system. It's a massive ball of hot plasma that generates energy through nuclear fusion. This energy supports life on Earth and drives our planet’s weather and climate. Composed mainly of hydrogen and helium, the Sun has a surface temperature of about 5,500°C (9,932°F) and an immense gravitational pull that keeps all the planets in orbit around it. The Sun is classified as a G-type main-sequence star (G dwarf), and it's about halfway through its 10-billion-year lifespan.
