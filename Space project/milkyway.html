The Milky Way Galaxy

The Milky Way is the galaxy that contains our Solar System. It is a barred spiral galaxy with an estimated 100 to 400 billion stars. Our Sun, and thus the Earth and our Solar System, are located in one of the spiral arms of the Milky Way known as the Orion Arm. The Milky Way has a diameter of about 100,000 light-years and is home to a diverse range of celestial bodies, including stars, planets, nebulae, and black holes. It's just one of billions of galaxies in the universe, and it's visible from Earth as a faint band of light stretching across the night sky.
