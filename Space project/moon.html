The Moon

The Moon is Earth's only natural satellite and the fifth largest moon in the Solar System. It has a diameter of 3,474 km and is about 384,400 km away from Earth. The Moonâ€™s gravitational pull causes the ocean tides on Earth and has been the target of space exploration since the 20th century.

The Moon goes through phases as it orbits Earth, ranging from new moon to full moon. It has no atmosphere, no weather, and extreme temperature fluctuations. In 1969, it became the first celestial body visited by humans when NASA's Apollo 11 landed astronauts on its surface.
