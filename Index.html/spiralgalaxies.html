Spiral Galaxies

Spiral galaxies are one of the most common types of galaxies in the universe. Characterized by their flat, rotating disks with central bulges and spiral arms, they are home to billions of stars, nebulae, and other cosmic objects.

Our own Milky Way is a classic example of a barred spiral galaxy. These galaxies typically have a central concentration of stars, known as the bulge, surrounded by a disk containing the spiral structure. The arms of a spiral galaxy are sites of active star formation and are often bright due to the presence of young, hot stars.

Spiral galaxies are classified further based on the tightness of their spiral arms and the size of the central bulge. They are found throughout the universe and play a vital role in our understanding of galactic evolution.
