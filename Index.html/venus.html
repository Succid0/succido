Venus

Venus is the second planet from the Sun and is the hottest planet in our solar system. It’s similar in size and structure to Earth but has a thick, toxic atmosphere filled with carbon dioxide and clouds of sulfuric acid, making it an extreme greenhouse planet.

Surface temperatures on Venus can reach up to 465°C (869°F), hotter than Mercury despite being farther from the Sun. Venus rotates very slowly and in the opposite direction of most planets, including Earth.

Due to its bright appearance and consistent presence in our skies, Venus is often called Earth's "sister planet" and was known to ancient civilizations as both the morning star and evening star.
